# Numismatic Glossary

## 1. Anatomy of a Coin
- **Obverse (Obv.):** The "heads" side, usually showing the portrait of the ruler or a primary deity.
- **Reverse (Rev.):** The "tails" side, showing a deity, personification, or historical scene.
- **Edge:** The outer rim of the coin (can be reeded, plain, or lettered).
- **Field:** The background space between the motifs and the legends.
- **Legend:** The text inscribed on the coin (usually in Latin, Greek, or Arabic).
- **Exergue (Ex.):** The space at the bottom of the reverse, often separated by a line, containing the mint mark or a date.
- **Motif:** The central image or theme (e.g., "Jupiter standing").
- **Flan (Blank):** The metal disk prepared for striking.
- **Die:** The engraved metal stamp used to strike coins.
- **Type:** The main design or motif on a coin.
- **Type Series:** A group of coins sharing the same basic design.

## 2. Condition & Surfaces
- **Patina:** The oxidation layer on the surface (valued on ancient bronzes). Can be green, brown, or blue-green.
- **Toning:** The iridescent or dark oxidation on silver coins (from "cabinet toning" in old collections).
- **Luster:** The original light-reflecting quality of the metal (on well-preserved coins).
- **Mint State (MS):** No wear, full luster - highest grade category.
- **About Uncirculated (AU):** Light friction on highest points, still has luster.
- **Extremely Fine (XF/EF):** Light wear on highest points, full design details visible.
- **Very Fine (VF):** Moderate wear, all major details clear.
- **Fine (F):** Heavy wear, but major features recognizable.
- **Good (G):** Very worn, design barely identifiable.
- **Poor (P):** Barely identifiable, often corroded.
- **Countermark:** A small stamp applied to a coin after its initial minting (to revalue or authorize in a different region).
- **Test Cut:** A small cut made to test the metal content (common on gold coins).

## 3. Mint Errors
- **Off-Center:** Coin struck outside the flan.
- **Double Strike:** Coin struck twice, causing double image.
- **Brockage:** Reverse die used as obverse, creating mirror image.
- **Mule:** Two different type designs combined (wrong dies paired).
- **Clipped:** Flan too small, missing part of design.
- **Cud:** Portion of design missing due to die break.
- **Die Crack:** Raised line on coin from die fracture.
- **Weak Strike:** Insufficient pressure, details not fully formed.

## 6. Mint Marks & Symbols
- **Mint Mark:** Letter or symbol indicating where a coin was minted.
- **Officina Mark:** Number indicating which workshop within a mint.
- **Exergue Mark:** Symbol in the exergue area.
- **Control Mark:** Secondary mark for quality control.
- **Symbol:** Religious or commemorative symbol (cross, star, moon).

## 7. Die & Variety Terminology
- **Die Variety:** Variations in die characteristics used to identify die links.
- **Die Link:** Two or more coins struck from the same die.
- **Die Axis:** Orientation of obverse to reverse (measured in hours).
- **Overstrike:** Coin minted over a previously existing coin.
- **Restrike:** Modern reproduction using original dies.
- **Copy:** Reproduction without original dies.

## 8. Metal & Alloy Terms
- **Aureus:** Roman gold coin (avg. 8g).
- **Denarius:** Roman silver coin (avg. 3-4g).
- **Antoninianus:** Roman silvered bronze coin (avg. 5g).
- **Sestertius:** Roman large brass (avg. 25g).
- **Dupondius:** Roman brass (avg. 12g).
- **As:** Roman copper (avg. 9g).
- **Quadrans:** Roman quarter-as (approx. 3g).
- **Billon:** Silver-copper alloy (low silver content).
- **Electrum:** Natural gold-silver alloy.
- **Orichalcum:** Ancient brass-like alloy.
- **Potin:** Bronze-tin alloy.
- **Tumbaga:** Gold-copper alloy (pre-Columbian).

## 9. Collection Types
- **Type Set:** One example of each type.
- **Date Set:** One example of each date/variety.
- **Mint Mark Set:** One example of each mint.
- **Die Variety Set:** One example of each die variety.
- **Grade Set:** All grades from Poor to MS.

## 10. Grading Services & Certifications
- **NGC (Numismatic Guaranty Corporation):** Major grading service.
- **PCGS (Professional Coin Grading Services):** Major grading service.
- **ANACS:** American Numismatic Association Certification Service.
- **ICCS (International Coin Certification Service):** Canadian grading service.
- **CAC (Certified Acceptance Corporation):** Market value verification label.
- **Cert Number:** Unique identifier on graded coins (e.g., "NGC 1234567").
