# Numismatic Glossary

## 1. Anatomy of a Coin
- **Obverse (Obv.):** The "heads" side, usually showing the portrait of the ruler or a primary deity.
- **Reverse (Rev.):** The "tails" side, showing a deity, personification, or historical scene.
- **Legend:** The text inscribed on the coin (usually in Latin, Greek, or Arabic).
- **Motif:** The central image or theme (e.g., "Jupiter standing").
- **Exergue (Ex.):** The space at the bottom of the reverse, often separated by a line, containing the mint mark or a date.
- **Field:** The background space between the motifs and the legends.
- **Flan (Blank):** The metal disk prepared for striking.

## 2. Condition & Surfaces
- **Patina:** The oxidation layer on the surface (valued on ancient bronzes).
- **Toning:** The iridescent or dark oxidation on silver coins.
- **Luster:** The original light-reflecting quality of the metal (on well-preserved coins).
- **Wear:** The loss of detail due to circulation (measured by grading systems like NGC/PCGS).
- **Countermark:** A small stamp applied to a coin after its initial minting (to revalue it or authorize it in a different region).

## 3. Latin/Greek Abbreviations
- **IMP:** Imperator (General/Emperor).
- **AVG:** Augustus (Majestic).
- **CAES:** Caesar.
- **COS:** Consul.
- **TR P:** Tribunicia Potestas (Tribunician Power).
- **PP:** Pater Patriae (Father of the Country).
- **S C:** Senatus Consulto (By Decree of the Senate).
- **P M:** Pontifex Maximus (High Priest).
- **S P Q R:** Senatus Populusque Romanus (The Senate and People of Rome).
